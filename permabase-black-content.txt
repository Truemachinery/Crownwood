Application Protocol
Permabase Black requires the exact same operational footprint as our standard stabilization formula. You receive maximum structural integrity and a premium asphalt finish simultaneously.

01. Catalytic Pigment Infusion
The proprietary carbon-black pigment is pre-mixed with the active Permabase bio-enzyme catalyst in the water truck. This ensures the colorant is carried deep into the soil profile along with the chemical activator.

02. Deep Lift Application
As the soil is bladed and compacted in lifts, the black-tinted enzyme saturates the entire depth of the treated subgrade (typically 6 to 12 inches), rather than just sitting superficially on the surface like an emulsion.

03. Hydrophobic Curing
The bio-enzyme collapses the clay lattice, expelling water and permanently binding the soil particles together into a dense, solid, water-repellent matrix infused with the carbon pigment.

04. UV-Resistant Finish
Once fully cured and compacted, the surface solidifies into a flat, dark, aesthetic finish. The carbon-black tint is highly UV resistant, holding its deep color significantly longer than untreated dirt.

Product Questions

01. Is Permabase Black a permanent replacement for asphalt?
Permabase Black is a permanent soil stabilizer that provides the aesthetic of asphalt. For low-speed, rural, or agricultural applications (like ranch roads or laydown yards), it is an incredible asphalt alternative. However, for high-traffic, high-speed commercial roads or areas experiencing severe kinetic stress, it should be used as the superior subgrade beneath a standard wearing course.

02. Will the black tint wash away in the rain?
No. The carbon-black tint is catalyzed directly into the soil matrix alongside the bio-enzyme. Because Permabase makes the treated soil permanently hydrophobic (water-repelling), the tint does not wash off, leach into the water table, or fade rapidly during rainstorms.

03. Can I use Permabase Black for dust control?
Yes. It is one of the most effective PM10 dust suppressants on the market. Unlike water spraying (which evaporates) or oil applications (which are toxic), Permabase Black chemically binds dust particles into a solid, dark, hardened surface that eliminates airborne particulate matter.

04. Does it cost more than standard Permabase?
There is a slight premium for the proprietary carbon-black pigment additive over our standard formula. However, the cost remains drastically lower than hauling off soil, pouring flex base, and paving a brand new asphalt road, saving projects an average of 40%.

Pigment Permanency
While highly UV resistant, the upper millimeter of the surface may lighten slightly over years of intense Texas sun exposure. For maintenance, a rapid, inexpensive surface re-application of the tint can instantly restore the deep black asphalt-like finish without requiring structural re-work.
