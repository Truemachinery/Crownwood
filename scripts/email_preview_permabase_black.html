Most counties are staring down the same problem: miles of failing dirt and chipseal roads, a shrinking road budget, and constituents who want pavement-grade results.

We have a product that closes that gap.

Permabase Black is a polymer emulsion base stabilizer that permanently hardens failing subgrades — and doubles as a prime coat and tack coat underseal before chipseal or asphalt paving. One 275-gallon tote covers approximately 1 mile of 20-foot-wide road at a finished cost of $1.96 per gallon.

That's roughly $5,400 per mile — no separate prime coat required, no additional mobilization.

 

Unlike traditional tack or prime emulsions, Permabase Black requires no heat. Apply it cold, straight out of whatever equipment you already have on hand — a water truck, tack wagon, asphalt distributor, or even a homemade water trailer with a spray bar and a trash pump. If it sprays water, it applies Permabase Black.

For comparison

•  Hot-mix asphalt: $80,000–$120,000+ per mile
•  Lime stabilization + asphalt cap: two mobilizations, double the cost
•  Calcium chloride dust control: temporary, reapply every season

Permabase Black stabilizes the base, seals it, and primes it for whatever wearing course comes next — chipseal or asphalt — all in a single pass. It also delivers permanent PM10 dust suppression and a dark, asphalt-like finish for low-speed roads that don't need a cap at all.
